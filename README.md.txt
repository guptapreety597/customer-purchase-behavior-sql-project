 Customer Purchase Behavior Analysis using MySQL

## Objective
Analyze customer purchasing patterns, revenue trends, and customer behavior using SQL.

## Dataset
The dataset contains customer demographics, purchase details, product categories, and transaction behavior such as:
- Customer ID
- Age, Gender
- Item Purchased
- Product Category
- Purchase Amount
- Location
- Size
- Color
- Season
- Review Rating
- Subscription Status
- Payment Method
- Shipping Type
- Discount Applied
- Promo Code Used
- Previous Purchases
- Preferred Payment Method
- Purchase Frequency

## SQL Analysis
Performed data analysis using MySQL including:
- Data cleaning and column standardization
- Null and duplicate checks
- Revenue analysis by category and location
- Customer segmentation (frequent, regular, occasional buyers)
- Purchase behavior analysis by gender, subscription status, and payment method
- Age group and category analysis

## Key Insights
- The Clothing category generates the highest revenue, contributing approximately $104,264 of total revenue, followed by Accessories.
- The top 10 customers contribute a significant portion of revenue, with the highest customer spending around $100.
- Frequent buyers have an average purchase of $59.99, which is slightly higher than occasional buyers ($59.96), indicating stronger customer engagement.
- The top-performing locations such as Montana ($5,784), Illinois ($5,617), and California ($5,605) generate the highest revenue, indicating strong regional demand concentration.
- PayPal (677 customers) and Credit Card (671 customers) are the most preferred payment methods, followed closely by Cash (670), indicating strong adoption of digital and card-based payments.
- The 55+ age group contributes the highest revenue, especially in the Clothing category ($31,504), followed by the 25–34 ($20,468) and 45–54 ($19,612) age groups, indicating higher spending among older customers.

## Conclusion
This project helps identify customer segments, high-performing categories, and purchasing patterns that can support better business decisions.
